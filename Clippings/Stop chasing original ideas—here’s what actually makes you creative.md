We often think of creativity as the need to invent something completely original. But the truth is, great art isn’t about invention — it’s about interpretation.  
  
In this video essay, we explore how some of the most iconic films and stories weren’t born from groundbreaking ideas, but from personal experience, emotional truth, and creative reinterpretation.  
  
Whether you’re a filmmaker, writer, or artist, this is for anyone who’s ever felt stuck chasing the next “big idea.” Creativity isn’t about what you invent — it’s about how you see what’s already there.
